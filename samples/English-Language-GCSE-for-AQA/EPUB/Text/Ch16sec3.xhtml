Write for purpose and audience

Look again at the first extract in Activity 1. The student seems to be telling a story, rather than writing a description. The only description given is of ‘the big clunky locks’ on the gates. By not describing, the student was not doing what they had been asked – they were not writing for purpose.

When you write something, always be clear about your purpose. Ask yourself what you are trying to achieve in your writing. You also need to know your audience – the person or people who will be reading your writing.

Activity 3

  1. 1

    Work with another student. Look at these different audiences and discuss how each one might influence the way you write:

    1. only you
    2. close friends
    3. other students who you do not know well
    4. people who will be judging you on how you write.

  2. 2

    Consider each of the following:

    1. an email to friends inviting them to a party
    2. a letter welcoming Year 1 students to primary school
    3. a letter to a newspaper about global warming.

    Discuss how the intended audience for each of these might affect the way it was written? Think about:

    • the sentence structures
    • the vocabulary choices
    • the importance of technical accuracy.

  3. 3

    Students A and B, whose work you read in Activity 1, were writing in an English Language exam. Their audience was the examiner. Which student is most likely to impress the examiner? Why do you think this?

  4. 4

    Work on your own. Write the first paragraph in response to the following exam task:

    Describe a city street at night.

    Remember:

    • your purpose is to describe a city street at night
    • your audience is an examiner who will assess you on your writing skills.

  5. 5

    Swap your writing with another student. Ask your partner to comment on how well you have written for purpose and audience.

  6. 6

    Use your partner's comments to help you redraft your paragraph. Think carefully about your range of sentence structures and your vocabulary choices. Check your final draft for technical accuracy.
