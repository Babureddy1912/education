Choose the tense for your writing

The students have both written in the past tense. To form the simple past tense, they have had to change the base form of the verbs they use. This base form is often called the infinitive. For example ‘to walk’ is the infinitive and ‘walked’ is the simple past tense. Make sure you know these rules for forming the simple past tense:

  • The past tense for many verbs is formed by adding ‘ed’ to the infinitive (e.g. to want wanted).
  • If the present tense of the verb ends in an ‘e’, it is normal to simply add ‘d’ (e.g. to decide decided; to settle settled).
  • If the present tense of the verb ends in a consonant and is preceded by a short vowel sound, it is normal to double the consonant and add ‘ed’ (e.g. to stun stunned; to rot rotted).
