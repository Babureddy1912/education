Chapter 2: Asides

Asides are block-level supplemental content that may present in a number of ways. They are broken into the following categories:

  • sidebar

  • footnote

  • endnote

  • marginalia

  • pullquote

  • annotation

1.1 Sidebars

A sidebar is a titled aside that is typically several paragraphs in length and may contain images and media and even small titled sections and a practice section at the end. It is typically thought of as a "boxed feature", owing to a common design treatment it receives to set it apart from the running text. It intrinsically floats; like a typical figure or table, it is allowed to be presented away from any text reference. Sidebars commonly have a label in addition to a title.

1.2 Footnotes

A footnote is an aside associated with a specific range of text. It is indicated by a marker in the text known as the footnote reference, typically a raised number or symbol. The presentation of the footnote itself is largely dependent on the presentation medium. In print, the footnote appears at the bottom of a page or of an object. In a digital product, the footnote reference is typically a hyperlink which may launch a popup window or re-direct the user to another location in the publication. The hyperlink to the footnote receives a special class, "noteref footnote", so that software applications and designers can treat them uniquely from other hyperlinks.

1.3 Endnotes

1.3.1 Chapter Endnotes

An endnote is an aside associated with a specific range of text. As with the footnote, it is indicated by a marker in the text known as the footnote reference, typically a raised number or symbol, and the presentation of the endnote itself is dependent on the presentation medium. Whereas a footnote in print appears at the bottom of a page or object, an endnote typically appears at the end of a chapter or the product itself. In a digital product, the endnote reference is a hyperlink which may launch a popup window or re-direct the user to another location in the publication. The hyperlink to the endnote receives a special class, "noteref endnote", so that software applications and designers can treat them uniquely from other hyperlinks. 1.4 Marginalia

Marginalia is an aside that is typically short in length, a single paragraph at most. Unlike a footnote or endnote, there is no visible marginalia reference in the text. Like a foonote, however, sometimes marginalia is highly connected to a span of text and is amplifying that text or commenting on it in some ways. The association with the text is usually communicated through vertically aligning the marginalia in the "margins" of the page with the text to which it corresponds. Other times, marginalia is more like a very small sidebar–somewhat related to the running text but not directly connected. In those cases, the marginalia has some flexibility as to where it positions. Many EPUB Reading Systems/CSS design do not have a good rendering model for marginalia, as some do not provide support for rendering a running text with a margin of art or text running alongside of it in the margins. Consequently the marginal comment ends up in the running text following a paragraph.
