3.3 marginalia

Marginalia is an anchor with a data-href attribute. It is not currently being applied as part of the spec. Its purpose is to provide a vertical alignment point in the text for a formatting program to reference for placement of the marginal note. Current CSS and Reading System makes no use of this capability so we have not required vendors to apply it. In some print composition workflows, the anchor is useful. We are currently evaluating whether to apply it in the print workflow.

3.4 Bibliographic reference

The United States becomes more ethnically and linguistically diverse every year. More than 90 percent of new residents come from non-English-speaking countries. The number of students with non-English-speaking backgrounds represents the fastest growing group of this population. In the last decade, the total student enrollment in public schools increased by only 14 percent, while the number of English learners grew 70 percent and is projected to grow even more (Abernathy, 2007). The 2000 U.S. Census identified 20 percent of school-age children as non-native English speakers).

The National League for Nursing (NLN) recognized that if nurses are to become effective users of the electronic health records, nurses need to be educated. The NLN called for reform to nursing education to promote quality education that prepares technologysavvy nurses who can use electronic health records. The NLN Board of Governors (2008) issued a position statement calling for both prelicensure and graduate nursing education programs to provide EHR education and hands on experiences for students.

Well-done and appropriate assessments are the basis for all successful special education and transition programming. In order to ensure appropriate testing practices, the IDEA addressed assessment concerns in the 1990, 1997, and 2004 amendments. The IDEA of 1990 required Individualized Education Program (IEP) assessments to be unbiased and given in the native language of the student. In addition, placement in special education was to be based upon a multifactored evaluation that included multiple assessments across more than one environment (C. K. Bennett, Journal of Staff Development, Winter 1984).

Footnote references on part, chapter, and section openers have their own subclass for when they must present differently than the footnotes in the rest of the part or chapter. In print, the footnote is sometimes "un-numbered" (not numbered, not symboled). We do not currently have a standard convention for how to render these un-numbered footnotes digitally so they are treated as a symboled footnote.

3.9 Webresource

A webresource represents Pearson trackable web asset specific to a Pearson product. Contrast webresource with a regular ulink. In the example below, www.myeconlab.com is the webresource. webresource maps to ulink in pxe basic

Log onto Pearson's www.myeconlab.com to learn more.

Note: A general Pearson website such as pearsoned.com is a marketing website so is a regular ulink, not a webresource.

3.12 Index-locator

Reference to a location within the body of the work but outside any index. See index chapter for this example.

3.12a termref

See Also cross-reference is wrapped in span epub:type="index-xref-related" and the index term being referenced should be marked using a hyperlink with class="termref" (e.g., a epub:type="termref" href="#index_advertising_1530". See index chapter for this example.

3.13 Obsolete URL

Used where the author refers to a website or page which is no longer accessible. Compare to ulink and url-example.

Cover Art: © Chin Yuen. www.chinyuenart.com The url is not tagged in the output because it links to a site that no longer exists. Ideally this content would be updated to a new url or the url would be removed so that the User doesn't wonder why some url's are hyperlinked and some are not. There is no formatting distinction for obsolete url's. It formats as normal text.
