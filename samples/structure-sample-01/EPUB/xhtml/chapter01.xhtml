Chapter 1: Lists

Lists are one of the most commonly used structures to represent author ideas, and they can appear in almost any context in PXE. PXE uses ol and ul to represent ordered and unorderedlists respectively and treats many Pearson structures as forms of an orderedlist.

The lists in this chapter are organized so that they appear in the contexts in which they would appear in a typical chapter.

Note Besides appearing in chapter frontmatter, bodymatter, and backmatter running text, lists also often appear nested in other structures, e.g., sidebars, figures, and tables. See the individual chapters on asides, figures, and tables for examples of lists formatted in those contexts.

1.5 Unordered Lists

Unorderedlists are expected to be displayed as one of the following:

  • un-numbered (meaning no numbers or bullets), class="nomark"

  • bulleted

The key characteristic of a ul is not so much that it is bulleted or un-numbered but that its order does not matter. If a list's order matters, then it should be marked as an ol, even if presentationally the author prefers no numbers or letters.
