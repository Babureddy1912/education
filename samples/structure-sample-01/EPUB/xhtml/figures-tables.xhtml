Chapter 2: Figures and Tables

2.1 Figure

An illustration, diagram, photo, etc, that is typically referenced from the main flow of the document and that can, without affecting the flow of the document, be moved away from that main flow, e.g. to the side of the page, or to dedicated pages.

Although the primary content of a figure is usually a graphic, the contents of a figure may also be textual or tabular.

2.2 Informalfigure

informalfigure is a class of figure that floats, is not referenced in the text, is usually a photograph, and is un-numbered.

2.3 Inline Figure

inline is a special class of figure that is required to be placed inline with running text, is always titled or captioned, is a graphic, and is not labeled or numbered. When a graphic is used alone with no caption, use the img element

2.4 Chapter or Part Opening Figure

opener is a class of figure that is used as part or chapter opening art. Opening art is often specially positioned relative to a design. It does not arbitrarily float, nor is it always inline. It often has a designated special position within the overall opening layout.

2.5 Figure containing a Table

When a table can float away from its main content without affecting flow or understanding of that content, table must be nested in figure. when table is nested inside of figure, the table title, caption, and source is coded as belonging to the containing figure

2.6 Informaltable

An informaltable is a table that must be set inline to the running text. It is typically un-numbered.
